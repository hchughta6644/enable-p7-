Understanding Accessibility & Neurodiversity

At ENABLE, we believe that accessibility is not a "bonus" feature—it is a fundamental right. Neurodiversity refers to the natural variation in human brains. This includes conditions such as Dyslexia, ADHD, Autism, and Dyscalculia.

What is Neurodiversity?

Neurodiversity is the idea that neurological differences are a natural part of human diversity. Instead of viewing these conditions as "deficits," we view them as different ways of processing information. University systems should be flexible enough to accommodate these different processing styles.

Common Conditions

  • Dyslexia: Affects reading and phonological processing.
  • ADHD: Affects executive function, focus, and emotional regulation.
  • Autism: Affects social communication and sensory processing.
  • Dyscalculia: Affects the ability to understand and process numbers.

Our Accessibility Features

Font Size Toggles

Why it helps: Many neurodiverse students experience visual stress or "crowding" when text is too small. Allowing users to increase font size reduces cognitive strain and improves focus.

Dark & Light Mode

Why it helps: Bright white screens can cause sensory overload for students with Autism or ADHD. Dark mode provides a calming, low-stimulation environment that is easier on the eyes during long study sessions.

High Contrast Mode

Why it helps: High contrast (Yellow on Black) is specifically designed for users with visual impairments or specific cognitive processing needs, ensuring that text stands out clearly from the background.

Dyslexia-Friendly Font

Why it helps: Fonts like OpenDyslexic use "bottom-heavy" characters to help readers distinguish between similar-looking letters (like 'b' and 'd'), preventing text from "floating" or rotating.
