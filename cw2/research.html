Research & Findings

The ENABLE project is rooted in academic research and user-centered design principles. Below we outline the core challenges identified in current university systems and our proposed benchmarks for success.

Challenges Faced by Neurodiverse Students

Cognitive Overload

Many university portals are cluttered with dozens of links, multiple menus, and conflicting visual styles. This leads to decision paralysis and anxiety for students with ADHD or high sensory sensitivity.

Information Scents

Current systems often hide critical information deep within nested folders. Poorly labeled buttons (e.g., "Click Here" vs "Download Assignment Brief") make navigation exhausting for students with processing delays.

Comparison: Current vs Ideal Accessible System

Feature Category Current University Systems Ideal Accessible System (ENABLE)
Customization Static color schemes and fixed font sizes. Full user control over themes, contrast, and typography.
Layout Dense, multi-column layouts with low white space. Simplified, single-column focus mode with high white space.
Navigation Hierarchical, deep levels of menu layers. Breadcrumbs, clear icons, and consistent global nav.
Visual Feedback Relies solely on color (e.g., red text for errors). Multi-modal feedback (Icons + Text + Color Cues).

Missing Features in Current IT Systems

Our review of major VLE (Virtual Learning Environment) systems found that while they comply with basic WCAG AA standards, they often fail to address neuro-specific needs:

  • No built-in dyslexia-friendly font options.
  • Lack of 'Calm Mode' to reduce visual distractions.
  • Inconsistent button designs across different modules.
